How it works

Four steps. You're always in control.

It works like AnyDesk, minus the account and the middle-man server. Nobody can reach your machine without the number you read them, and nothing happens on your screen until you say yes.

01

Open CEC Support. It shows you a short Support number — that's it. Nothing connects; you're simply reachable to whoever you give the number to.

02

Read us the number. On the phone or in chat, tell your CEC technician your number. Only someone with it can even find your computer — the number is your private, one-off doorway.

03

We ask to connect. A box pops up: “<your technician> is trying to connect to your computer,” with a short code you can confirm with us out loud.

04

You choose how long. Approve Once, Auto-Approve for 3 hours, or Auto-Approve Forever — then we can see and control your screen to fix it. Change your mind any time: revoke and we're gone instantly.

Is it safe?

Built so you never have to just trust us.

CEC Support rides the same private mesh technology as our AllMyStuff app. There is no server in the middle holding your screen or your files — the connection is straight from your machine to your technician's, end-to-end encrypted.

Per-session or general

Approve just this session, a 3-hour window for a repair, or standing access — your call, every time.

Revocable instantly

“Forget this technician” cuts the connection on the next frame — no waiting, no calling us to ask.

Encrypted & direct

End-to-end encrypted, peer-to-peer. No cloud relay stores your session; nobody in the middle can watch.

Nothing exposed

No open ports, no public address. Without your number, your machine is simply invisible.

Tiny footprint

One small app. Close it and you're off the mesh — unless you asked us to stay for an unattended repair.

Open source

The whole thing is on GitHub, MIT-licensed. Read it, or have someone you trust read it.

For longer repairs

Unattended, only if you want it.

Some fixes need a reboot — or three. If you pick Auto-Approve for 3 hours or Forever, you can also let CEC Support install itself as a background service, so we can reconnect after a restart and keep going without you sitting there. It's the same “unattended access” an app like AnyDesk offers — except it's your choice, it's time-boxed unless you said Forever, and one click on your end ends it. On a machine that already runs AllMyStuff, CEC Support quietly reuses it rather than installing anything extra.
