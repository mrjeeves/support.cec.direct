No surprises

What the installer actually does.

  1. Runs the canonical installer maintained in the app repo — support.cec.direct/install.ps1 is just the short URL for it, so the site can't drift from what the app ships.
  2. Installs the CEC Support app for your machine from the latest GitHub release, verifying every SHA-256 sidecar.
  3. Reuses, doesn't clobber. If you already run our AllMyStuff app, CEC Support quietly reuses its mesh components instead of installing duplicates. If you don't, it brings its own — you never need to have heard of AllMyStuff.
  4. Adds -Service (Windows) or --service (macOS/Linux) to install the background service for an unattended repair. Leave it off and CEC Support only runs while it's open.

No telemetry, no account. Close the app and you're off the mesh.

Verify anything

Checksums ship with every asset.

Every archive has a .sha256 sidecar in the release. On Windows:

verify a downloadpowershell
PS> Get-FileHash .\cec-support-windows-x86_64.zip -Algorithm SHA256

macOS / Linux: shasum -a 256 -c or sha256sum -c. Compare against the .sha256 in the release. The source is on GitHub, MIT.

After you install

Then it's just three things.

01

Open it and read us your number. The app shows a short Support number. Tell it to your technician.

02

Approve the connection. When the “trying to connect” box appears, confirm the name and code, then pick Approve Once / 3 hours / Forever.

03

Revoke when you're done. Hit “Forget this technician” any time to end access instantly.
