Questions

Frequently asked.

Short, plain answers. Still stuck? Your CEC technician can walk you through any of it.

What is CEC Support?

A small app that lets a Critical Error Computing technician see and control your computer to fix problems — like AnyDesk, but over a private, encrypted, direct connection with no account and no company server sitting in the middle. It only ever connects when you allow it.

What can the technician actually see?

Only while you have an active, approved session: your screen, and — if you granted control — your keyboard and mouse. Nothing before you approve, and nothing after you revoke. There is no background “always watching”; you see a banner the whole time someone is connected.

How do I stop or revoke access?

Click Disconnect to end the current session, or Forget this technician to remove their access entirely. Revoking takes effect immediately — the screen and control stop on the next frame, even mid-session. You never have to call us to be sure you're disconnected.

What are the three approval options?

Approve Once allows just this one session and is forgotten as soon as it ends. Auto-Approve for 3 hours lets us reconnect without re-asking during a repair, then expires on its own. Auto-Approve Forever keeps standing access until you revoke it. All three are revocable at any time.

Does it keep running after I close it, or after a reboot?

By default, no — close the app and you're off the mesh, unreachable. It only stays available across restarts if you chose “3 hours” or “Forever” and allowed it to install a background service for an unattended repair. Even then, it's your choice, it's time-boxed unless you picked Forever, and revoking ends it.

Does CEC store my screen or my files?

No. The connection is peer-to-peer and end-to-end encrypted — the video of your screen goes straight from your machine to your technician's. There is no cloud relay recording or keeping your session, and no files are uploaded anywhere.

What's the “number,” and can someone guess it?

It's a short code your app shows you and you read to us. It's how a technician even finds your computer on the shared network — without it, your machine is invisible. Knowing the number only lets someone ask to connect; they still can't do anything until you approve them and confirm the on-screen code. So the number is a private doorway, and your approval is the lock.

Do I need an account?

No account, no sign-up, no card. Download, open, read us the number.

What platforms does it run on?

Windows first (10 and 11). macOS and Linux builds are on the download page too.

How is this different from AllMyStuff?

AllMyStuff links your own computers together so you can reach them yourself. CEC Support is a one-tap link to our help desk — it's for letting a CEC technician in to fix something, then getting them back out. If you already run AllMyStuff, CEC Support reuses it instead of installing anything extra.

How do I uninstall it?

Remove it like any app (Windows: Settings → Apps, or the uninstaller). If you'd installed the background service, turn it off first in the app's settings — or uninstalling removes it too. Once it's gone, your machine is no longer reachable.

Windows warns about an unknown publisher — is that a problem?

Early builds may show a SmartScreen or “unknown publisher” prompt until our code-signing reputation builds up. Only ever download CEC Support from support.cec.direct or our GitHub releases, and check the .sha256 if you want to be certain.
